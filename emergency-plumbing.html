What to Do During a Plumbing Emergency in Providence

While our team is on the way to your Providence home, take these steps to minimize damage:

  • Shut off the main water supply — typically in the basement near the front of the house or outside in the meter pit.
  • Turn off electricity in areas where water is present or near the electrical panel if flooding is near it.
  • Document the damage — photos and videos will help with any insurance claims.
  • Move valuables out of affected areas if safe to do so.
  • Stay on the line with our dispatcher — they can walk you through additional steps specific to your situation.

Emergency Plumbing FAQ — Providence RI

How fast can you get to my Providence home for a plumbing emergency?

Our standard emergency response time in Providence, RI is 60 minutes or less from the time you call. We maintain emergency plumbers on-call throughout the Providence metro area 24 hours a day, 7 days a week to achieve this response time. What counts as a plumbing emergency?

A plumbing emergency is any situation where immediate action is needed to prevent serious property damage, health hazards, or loss of essential services. This includes: burst or actively leaking pipes, sewage backup inside the home, no water to the entire property, major flooding from plumbing failures, gas line leaks, sump pump failure during heavy rain, and water heater failures that create safety hazards. If you're unsure, call us — we'll help you assess the situation for free.

Do you charge more for emergency calls at night or on weekends?

Yes, there is an emergency after-hours dispatch surcharge for calls outside of standard business hours. This fee will be clearly stated by our dispatcher before we send anyone out. Once you agree to the service call, we provide a flat-rate quote for the repair work — the labor rate for the actual repair does not change based on time of day.

Can you help me file an insurance claim after a plumbing emergency?

Yes. Our team can provide detailed documentation including photos, written reports, and itemized invoices that your homeowner's insurance company will need. We have experience working alongside insurance adjusters in Providence and can often communicate directly with your insurance company to streamline the claims process.
