Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from Providence RI homeowners about our plumbing services.

How quickly can you get to my home in Providence RI for a plumbing emergency?

For plumbing emergencies anywhere in Providence, Rhode Island, our typical response time is 60 minutes or less — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, including holidays. We keep emergency plumbers on-call around the clock because we know plumbing disasters don't follow business hours. Call us at (401) 555-5825 for immediate assistance.

Do you charge extra for after-hours or weekend plumbing calls in Providence?

We're transparent about our pricing. Emergency after-hours service calls do carry a slightly higher dispatch fee than standard business-hours calls, but we'll tell you the exact price upfront before you commit. For planned work, we offer appointments 7 days a week with no extra weekend charges.

Are your plumbers licensed in Rhode Island?

Absolutely. All of our plumbers hold current Rhode Island plumbing licenses issued by the RI Department of Labor and Training. Our company holds master plumber license #PL-04482. We are also fully bonded and insured, protecting you and your Providence home throughout every job.

What plumbing services do you offer in Providence RI?

We offer a complete range of residential and commercial plumbing services in Providence including: emergency plumbing, drain cleaning & hydro-jetting, water heater repair and installation (tank and tankless), pipe repair and replacement, sewer line services, bathroom and kitchen fixture installation, gas line services, and whole-home repiping. See our full services page for details.

How much does a plumber in Providence RI cost?

Plumbing costs in Providence vary by service type and complexity. Simple repairs like fixing a leaky faucet typically run $95–$250. Drain cleanings range from $150–$350. Water heater replacements typically run $900–$2,500 depending on the unit. We always provide written flat-rate quotes before starting work — no surprises. Contact us for a free estimate on your specific project.

Do you work on older homes in Providence?

Yes — and we specialize in it. Providence has a large stock of older Victorian and Colonial-era homes, many with original or aging plumbing. Our team is experienced with older pipe materials including galvanized steel, cast iron, and clay sewer pipes. We understand the unique challenges of Providence's historic housing and can bring your plumbing up to modern standards while respecting the character of your home.
